About Me

Yale School of Art

I took a human computer interaction class during my freshman year at Drexel University. For our final project, we were tasked with redesigning a website that we felt could look better than it does at the moment. Our group decided on redesigning the Yale School of Art’s website, since we felt that the site wasn’t very visually pleasing which surprised us because it’s an art school’s website.

Above is how the homepage looked, and still looks like on their website. We felt that the highlighted titles were very distracting to the viewer, as well as the background animations. Yes, the entire background of the homepage as well as the other pages on the website are animated. Another design choice that we weren’t fans of were the colored boxes, both solid and gradient, that surround most of the text and images on the website. We felt like the colors clashed with each other, that they were distracting to the user, and that the content could be displayed in a much simpler, modern way than they were at the moment.

All 4 people in my group created accounts on usertesting.com so we could collect feedback from other people who visited the site. We sent the link to the website, as well as various general questions about the layout and user interface of the website. Each of us collected feedback from 3 users and designed the website based on this feedback.

I was specifically in charge of designing the footer of the site, as well as the navigation bar on the side. I chose to have the navigation bar scroll with the user so that they would not have to scroll back to the top of the page to go to another section of the site. For the footer, I tried spacing out all of the options as to have it feel as simple and easy to understand as possible. I also helped out with general spacing of elements as well as the organization of content for the overall redesign.

Part of the assignment was to add a few interactions to the website to demonstrate how the flow of the design would feel. The first interaction we added was to the search bar, which expands when a user clicks on it.

The second interaction we added was to the login button at the top right of the site. This interaction consisted of a login menu appearing under the login button when a user clicks on it.

The third interaction was added to an article near the bottom of the page. We programmed the “Read More” clickable option to take the user to another page, where the full article would be displayed. We also added the option to either click the “Home” tab on the navigation bar or the title of the website at the top of the page to go back to the homepage.

The overall experience with working on a team to design a website was a lot of fun. We all have our different design preferences and thoughts, so it was really nice to be able to come together and combine ideas to create something that we are all very proud of. I’d say that this was my favorite project to work on during my freshman year at Drexel University.
